Defenses

Defenses agains the Ducky can take several forms, though each can be bypassed with some effort, so a combination of several methods is necessary to ensure security.

Physical Access

The Ducky requires physical access to the target machine, or specifically, a USB port on the target machine. It doesn't have to be consistent access, just long enough for the script to run and the attacker to remove or hide the Ducky. Physical security is necessary to prevent an attack from gaining illicit access to the machine, and training against social engineering will help prevent more sophisticated attackers from talking their way into access.

Adam Tigar and Ben Stone, Rubber Ducky USB
