References

usbrubberducky.com

The home of the ducky, basic details and examples, and a variety of useful links.

The best way to gain an appreciation of the impact of the Rubber Ducky is to look at some of the payloads you can include on it. Here are some of the top sites for premade payloads or ways to generate your own.

ducktoolkit.com

This is a fairly basic and easily configurable site for building payloads, and provides a glimpse at the possibilities that exist, such as capturing system and configuration details, conducting network and port scans, and emailing information home or saving it back to the ducky.

Simple Ducky Payload Generator

This is a more advanced payload generator, which can integrate with Netcat and Meterpreter, extract and crack passwords, and more.

Rubber Ducky Wiki Payloads

Here's a list of interesting payloads which are premade for you. These can get more malicious, and even be starting points for different attacks, such as DNS poisoning, disabling antivirus, or installing backdoors.
