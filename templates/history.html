History

First Keyboards

In 1986, IBM released their first PC equipped with a keyboard, the Model M. With it came the PS/2 port specifically made for keyboard and mouse inputs on PCs. These devices often requried specific drivers for the PS/2 port to function. Without them the port registers no input.

USB - Universal Serial Bus

Changed the use of ports for computer peripherals immensley. Though current computers (such as Apple's Macbook Pro) have migrated to the slimmer USB-C, basic USB 2.0/3.0 is found on most devices. These devices subscribe to USB Human Interface Device Compliance, which defines the how computers connect to USB devices.
